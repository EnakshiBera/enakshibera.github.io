Enakshi Bera

PhD Candidate in Economics · Indiana University Bloomington

I am a fourth-year PhD student in Economics at Indiana University Bloomington. My research lies at the intersection of international trade and agricultural economics, with a focus on how market access, input costs, and output prices shape farmers' production decisions. I study the effectiveness of government fertilizer subsidies in influencing input adoption and whether these policies promote efficient use or contribute to over-application.

In related work, I examine how land fragmentation and economic stress affect farmer welfare in India, with particular attention to the mechanisms linking small-scale farming, income volatility, and suicide rates.
